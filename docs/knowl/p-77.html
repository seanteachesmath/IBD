Looking at our definition of \(r=b-m\text{,}\) if the mortality rate outweighs the birth rate the net, or intrinsic, growth rate is negative (in these cases it is sometimes useful to explicitly write a negative sign in the differential equation). Otherwise, if births outweigh mortality, it is positive. Like before, we will plot the right hand side of our model as a function of the population size. Rather than calling it a ’cobwebbing diagram’ we will call this a ‘phase-line diagram’. Unlike before this is a graph of “change vs. size” not “next vs. now.”
