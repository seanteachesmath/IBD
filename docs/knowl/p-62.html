Counter to our intuition, ‘closed form’ solutions to interesting models are often impossible to find or are uselessly complex when found. If we seek numerical solutions or graphs of analytical solutions, we need precise numerical values for initial conditions and parameters. For example, when applying our qualitative methods to our emigration model, we found that \(N^* = \dfrac{g}{r}\text{.}\) This analysis require little, if any, assumptions on \(g\) and \(r\) other than that they were ‘some positive values.’
