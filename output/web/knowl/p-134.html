To find \(B\)-nullclines, we set \(0 = B\left(r \left(1-\dfrac{B}{K}\right) - p C\right)\) to get either \(B = 0\) or \(C = \frac{r}{p} \left(1-\dfrac{B}{K}\right)\text{.}\) The nontrivial nullcline is a line with a vertical intercept of \(\frac{r}{p}\) and a horizontal intercept of \(B=K\) in the \(BC\)-plane.
