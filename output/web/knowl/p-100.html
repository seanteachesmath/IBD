To understand interspecific competition, we need to understand the units and magnitude of \(\mu\) and \(\delta\text{.}\) Roughly, \(\mu\) translates from units of beetle density to an “effective ant density.” According to the ratio \(\frac{B}{K_A}\text{,}\) beetles ‘fill’ some of the carrying capacity allotted to ants. Just how much of the capacity that they fill is given by the parameter \(\mu\text{,}\) which when multiplied by \(B\) gives the ‘ant equivalency’ of \(B\) beetles. We will look more closely at the equation for \(B\text{,}\) but note that we could make similar observations about the equation for \(A\text{.}\)
