Notice the peculiarity of the black line (or curve) in the right panel of Figure 6.2.1(or Figure 6.3.2). It quite happily ‘goes negative’, which means for an initially large enough population, the subsequent generation is multiplied by a negative number. Simply stated, that is weird. Fortunately, our other models circumvent this problem by requiring \(p.c.p.>0\) at all population sizes.
