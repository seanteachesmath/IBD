Where opposite nullclines cross, we have equilibria, but the number of equilibria depends on how \(K\) and \(\frac{m}{\epsilon p}\) compare. If \(K \lt \frac{m}{\epsilon p}\text{,}\) the \(B\)-nullcline crosses the horizontal axis before intersecting the vertical \(C\)-nullcline and there are only two equilibria, at \((B^*, C^*) = (0, 0)\) and \(B^*, C^*) = (K, 0)\text{.}\) Otherwise, if \(K \gt \frac{m}{\epsilon p}\text{,}\) the \(B\)-nullcline crosses the vertical \(C\)-nullcline before intersecting the horizontal axis at \(K\) and a third equilibrium emerges, at \((B^*, C^*) = \left(\dfrac{m}{\epsilon p}, \dfrac{r}{p}\left(1-\dfrac{m}{\epsilon p K}\right)\right)\text{.}\) The previous equilibria remain.
