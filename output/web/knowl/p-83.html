Since the total number* of individuals \(N\) is preserved, we can replace the equation for \(S\) by an algebraic constraint \(S=N-I\text{.}\) We can substitute this into the equation for \(I\) and derive a 1-dimensional, nonlinear model,
\begin{equation*} \frac{d^{} {I}}{d {t}^{}}=\beta I (N-I)-\delta I. \end{equation*}
If we define our model \(h(I)=\beta I (N-I)-\delta I,\) we can plot the more interesting version of our phase line as in [cross-reference to target(s) "fig-phase" missing or not unique]. Ignoring graphs for the moment, we can also compute equilibria of continuous time models, defined as solutions to \(h(I^*)=0\text{.}\) This gives \(I^*=0\) and \(I^*=N-\frac{\delta}{\beta}.\) It might prove useful to notice that our nontrivial solution exists when \(\delta<\beta N\) this suggests a limitation on the recovery rate that depends on the contact parameter \(\beta\) and the total population size \(N\text{.}\)
