Consider
\begin{equation*} \begin{cases} \dfrac{dS}{dt} \amp = \gamma I - \beta S I\\ \dfrac{dI}{dt} \amp = \beta S I - \gamma I \end{cases} \end{equation*}
This model tracks the populations of individuals in two classes of a population of size \(N\text{.}\) Since there are only two classes, \(N = S+ I\text{.}\) Differentiating gives
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{dN}{dt} = \dfrac{dS}{dt} + \dfrac{dI}{dt} = 0, \end{equation*}
in words, the total population size is constant. We can actually replace one of our differential equations with an algebraic constraint given by \(S = N - I\text{.}\) This gives us a chance to write our 2D system in 1D, meaning that this model may not actually require any of our new tools. We have
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{dI}{dt} = \beta (N-I)I-\gamma I. \end{equation*}
With a little rewriting, this becomes
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{dI}{dt} = \beta N I\left(1-\frac{I}{N}\right)-\gamma I\text{.} \end{equation*}
Looking closely, aside from the letters being different, this is exactly the form of the logistic growth model with proportional harvesting from [cross-reference to target(s) "logistic-prop-harvest" missing or not unique]. We could completely analyze this with steps that parallel our work back there. The threshold quantity for stability relates the parameters \(\beta\text{,}\) \(N\text{,}\) and \(\gamma\text{.}\) Note that \(N\) wasn’t originally in our model, but was introduced in the steps to eliminate the equation for \(S\text{.}\) There was no reason we would have known to do this, but it turned out that there was no reason we couldn’t do it.
