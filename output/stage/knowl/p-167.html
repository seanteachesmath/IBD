Instead, if we multiply the second equation by \(\nicefrac{1}{2}\text{,}\) we get \(\nicefrac{1}{2}(2x+6y) =\nicefrac{1}{2}(20)\) which is \(x+3y = 10\text{.}\) Subtract that from both sides of the first equation, \begin{alignat*} 5x+3y \amp= \amp\amp\phantom{-}26\\ - (x+3y) \amp\phantom{=} \amp\amp-10\\ \hline 4x - 0y \amp= \amp\amp16 \end{alignat*} Notice this completely eliminates the \(y\)-variable and we have \(x = 4\) as the solution. Substituting this back in to each original equation for \(x\) we find \(y = 2\text{.}\) Alternatively we could have multiplied the second equation by \(\nicefrac{5}{2}\) and used that to eliminate \(y\) and solve for \(y\text{.}\)
