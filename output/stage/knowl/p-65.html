For real-valued solutions we need \(\frac{K^2}{4} - \frac{hK}{r} \gt 0\) or \(\frac{rK}{4} \gt h\text{,}\) in words, \(h\) must be small. When \(h = 0\text{,}\) the equilibria are \(N^*=0\) and \(N^* = K\) (consistent with the basic logistic growth model). When \(h = \frac{rK}{4}\text{,}\) the equilibria collide at \(N^*=\frac{K}{2}\text{.}\) In between, equilibria are described by the upper and lower halves of a sideways parabola that shares these three points in common.
