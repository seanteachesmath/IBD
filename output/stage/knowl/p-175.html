Now for a twist. Assume that plants experience variable weather, specifically alternating wet and dry years. For example, \(P_1 = M_wP_0\text{,}\) followed by \(P_2 = M_dP_1\text{.}\) We might use the 2-year updating matrix \(M_{dw} = M_d M_w\) and measure our plants every two years. Let \(Z_t\) denote the \(t\)-th measurement (i.e., made so that \(Z_0 = P_0\text{,}\) \(Z_1 = P_2\text{,}\) \(Z_2 = P_4\text{,}\) and so on), described by
\begin{equation*} Z_{t+1} = M_{dw} Z_t\text{.} \end{equation*}
  • Compute a few values of the solution from initial conditions \(V_0 = 100\) and \(R_0 = 0\text{.}\)
  • Compute eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the matrix \(M_{dw}\text{.}\)
  • Express the solution for these initial conditions in terms of eigenvectors and eigenvalues.
  • What do you notice about high powers of each eigenvalue?
